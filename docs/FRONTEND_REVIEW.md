# Run Lighter front-end review

## What was working

- The core line, “Your business should not need you everywhere,” is clear and emotionally relevant to owner-led businesses.
- The offer ladder reduces risk by starting with an on-site review and a proof sprint.
- The automation-log example explains the service more clearly than abstract AI imagery would.
- The page has a coherent structure, repeated calls to action and sensible mobile stacking.

## Improvements implemented

1. **Made the first screen distinctive.** The original cream hero was clean but could have belonged to almost any consultancy. It now uses original, grounded Australian business photography and puts the owner outcome first.
2. **Refined the colour system.** Deep Harbour and Carbon now carry the premium weight; Warm Paper keeps the page human; coral is reserved for decisions and calls to action.
3. **Reduced the opening copy.** The hero now explains what Run Lighter does in one sentence without listing every possible automation.
4. **Moved the automation log into its own proof section.** It is easier to understand when framed as “what lighter looks like,” rather than competing with the main promise.
5. **Added visual pacing.** Original images now break up the problem, method and audience sections so the page does not become a long wall of text.
6. **Made the offer more credible.** Copy consistently says the first process is proved before a wider build and avoids invented results or case studies.
7. **Fixed form failure handling.** The booking form only shows success after a successful response and gives a direct email fallback when delivery fails.
8. **Improved accessibility and mobile behaviour.** Added a keyboard skip link, better mobile CTA stacking, descriptive image text and reduced-motion support.
9. **Improved sharing and search metadata.** Added a canonical address, absolute social-image references and X/social-card metadata.

## Recommended next improvements after launch data

- Replace the generic founder monogram with a strong, natural photo of Adrian in a real client or workshop environment.
- Add the first measurable case study as soon as a founding-client sprint is complete.
- Add a scheduling calendar only after the form has produced enough qualified leads to justify reducing the human qualification step.
- Connect the final `.com.au` domain after the name and trade mark checks are complete.
- Add privacy and terms pages before scaling paid traffic.
