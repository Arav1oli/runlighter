# Run Lighter master business and brand plan

Updated: 20 July 2026

## 1. Business in one sentence

Run Lighter helps growing, owner-led Sydney businesses remove repeated digital work by designing and building practical automation around the tools they already use.

## 2. Brand foundation

### Name

**Run Lighter**

The name expresses the emotional and operational outcome. The business becomes easier to run, less dependent on the owner and less weighed down by manual work.

### Core promise

**Your business should not need you everywhere.**

### Descriptor

**Practical automation for growing businesses.**

### Tagline

**Better systems. Less manual work.**

### USP

**On-site automation built around how your business actually runs.**

Run Lighter does not sell a generic AI package. It visits the business, maps the real workflow, finds the strongest opportunity, builds one useful system and proves it before expanding.

### Point of view

- AI should not be added everywhere.
- Repeated digital work should not rely on memory.
- Human judgement, relationships and accountability stay human.
- Existing software should be improved and connected before it is replaced.
- Start with the process causing the most friction.
- Prove value before recommending a larger build.

## 3. Ideal customer

### Primary market

Profitable, owner-led, sales-driven service businesses in Sydney with approximately 10 to 50 staff.

### Buyer

The owner, managing director, general manager or operations leader.

### Business conditions

- High-value enquiries
- Several software platforms
- Repetitive administration
- Slow lead follow-up
- Staff copying data between systems
- Reports rebuilt manually
- Growth that has outpaced internal processes
- An owner involved in too many approvals, handovers and reminders

### Priority sectors

Run Lighter remains sector-flexible, but the strongest early audiences are:

1. Professional and advisory services
2. Trades and field services
3. Sales-led businesses
4. Agencies and marketing teams
5. Established specialist service businesses

## 4. What Run Lighter sells

### On-Site Automation Review

Free during the founding launch.

- Ninety minutes at the client's business
- Current workflow map
- Top three automation opportunities
- Plain-English recommendation the owner keeps

### Proof Sprint

From AUD $4,500.

- One valuable automation
- Built around existing software
- Put live within weeks
- Result measured
- Credited towards a larger build where appropriate

### Core Systems Build

AUD $15,000 to $35,000.

- Connected lead flow
- Administration and operations workflows
- Reporting and visibility
- Staff handover and support

### Managed Automation Partnership

Target first-year value: approximately AUD $30,000.

A workable model is AUD $12,000 implementation followed by AUD $1,500 per month for twelve months.

## 6. Current brand identity

### Canonical colour palette

| Role | Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary dark | Moss | `#344137` | Headlines, dark canvases, buttons |
| Primary mid | Eucalyptus | `#587664` | Active states, diagrams, icons |
| Supporting green | Sage | `#718069` | Secondary accents and labels |
| Soft surface | Sage Wash | `#DFE4D7` | Panels, cards, pale backgrounds |
| Main canvas | Oat | `#F3EFE5` | Primary page and artwork background |
| Raised surface | Warm Paper | `#FBF8F1` | Cards and form surfaces |
| Neutral line | Stone | `#D8D1C4` | Borders and separators |
| Body ink | Deep Ink | `#29312C` | Long-form text |

Bright orange is not part of the current brand. Social artwork should remain green, oat, paper and stone.

### Typography

- Website display: DM Sans, medium and semibold
- Website body: Manrope
- Campaign artwork: Archivo, bold to black
- Fallback: Arial or system sans-serif

### Visual character

Practical, calm, premium, earthy, direct and technically capable.

Use:

- Clear workflow diagrams
- Cards, paths, nodes, timelines and process maps
- Familiar business interfaces without copying real software
- Normal Australian business settings when photography is needed
- Soft natural surfaces and restrained shadows
- Large headlines with strong spacing
- Minimal use of human figures

Avoid:

- Robots
- Holograms
- Glowing AI brains
- Neon or sci-fi imagery
- Futuristic dashboards
- Bright orange
- Glaring colour combinations
- Generic stock-photo distress poses
- Sports cars, trains or speed clichés
- Profit claims
- More than seven words on artwork
- Em dashes in public-facing copy
- Invented business names or logos

## 7. Voice and messaging

### Tone

Direct, grounded, calm, useful and owner-to-owner.

### Language rules

- Lead with a recognisable business problem.
- Explain the operational result before mentioning technology.
- Prefer automation, workflow, follow-up, reporting and systems over AI jargon.
- Never imply that every process should use AI.
- Never present staff removal as the main benefit.
- Do not discuss profit in advertising.
- Keep artwork to one bold thought with seven words or fewer.
- Put explanation and CTA in the caption.

### Reusable messages

- Your business should not need you everywhere.
- Better systems. Less manual work.
- We automate repeated work, not judgement.
- Start with the business, not the technology.
- Start small. Prove it. Then go deeper.
- Find your first useful automation.

## 10. Organic before advertising

The native pages should demonstrate three things before ads begin:

1. Run Lighter understands real operational friction.
2. The brand has a consistent and credible point of view.
3. The on-site review is a practical, low-risk first step.

The first content grid intentionally mixes urgency, education, operational pain and the discovery offer. Organic engagement is useful, but the main purpose is credibility when an ad prospect checks the profile.

## 11. Advertising test structure

### Round 1: Problem

Test distinct owner motivations with the same CTA.

- Falling behind
- Staff capacity
- Slow follow-up
- Manual data movement
- Owner dependency

### Round 2: Audience

Adapt the strongest two messages for professional services, trades, sales-led businesses and agencies.

### Round 3: Visual execution

Test the same message as:

- Bold typographic artwork
- Grounded business photography
- Simple operational metaphor

### Round 4: Offer

Test:

- Book an On-Site Automation Review
- Find Your First Useful Automation
- Get Your Automation Opportunity Map

Judge success in this order:

1. Qualified booked reviews
2. Completed lead forms
3. Landing-page conversion
4. Cost per qualified lead
5. Click-through rate

## 12. Proof and responsibility

Until case studies exist, Run Lighter should not claim specific savings or outcomes. Credibility comes from the on-site process, clear scope, small first build and measurable proof.

Privacy, access control, data flow, human oversight and ongoing monitoring are part of implementation, not optional extras.

## 13. Next commercial milestone

The next milestone is not a larger content library. It is the first qualified on-site review, followed by a Proof Sprint that produces a measurable case study.
